# Competitor Research — One-Page Small Business Websites (2026)

**Date:** 2026-06-02 (GK planning + research phase)  
**Purpose:** Inform design, copy, UX, pricing, and trust signals for eternalwebsite.com one-pager. Focus on premium-but-not-flashy, local-service, trustworthy feel for small businesses (restaurants, law, trades, consultants, clinics).

**Sources:** web_search + web_fetch on live sites. Citations use render_inline_citation where factual claims appear in Report.md.

## 1. Plumbing Gurus (plumbgurus.com) — Service / Trades Example
- **URL:** https://www.plumbgurus.com/
- **Strengths:**
  - Immediate trust/lead: Big phone number + "CALL NOW ANY JOB, WE WILL FIX IT" + prominent "GET A FREE QUOTE!" CTA at very top.
  - Services: 5 icon+short-desc cards (Bathrooms, Kitchens, Water Heaters, Sewer, Gas Piping) — scannable, benefit-oriented.
  - Local proof: "Plumbing Gurus LLC is a residential plumbing service located in Lindenhurst Illinois." + mission + "aspiring to continue growing throughout Northern Illinois."
  - Social proof: 2 named testimonials with specific stories (water heater Sunday/Monday, honesty).
  - Contact bottom: phone, email, counties served, social icons, repeated quote CTA.
- **Tone:** Direct, reliable, no-nonsense. Good for local trades.
- **Takeaways for Eternal:** Repeat CTA (top + services + contact). Icon grids work for services. Named local testimonials > generic. List served areas explicitly. Phone + quote button in hero is powerful for lead gen.
- **Citations:** [web:4] from one-page examples search (Wix blog).

## 2. Mazzey Law (mazzeylaw.com) — Professional Services / Law (Boutique)
- **URL:** https://www.mazzeylaw.com/
- **Strengths:**
  - Hero: "BOUTIQUE LAW FIRM — Providing Legal Services for Washington, D.C. & Virginia Small Businesses and Startups". Clear niche + value prop.
  - Services: Clean bulleted list (Legal Guidance, Employment Agreements, NDAs, Contract Review, LLC Formation) — no clutter.
  - Vision/About: Strongly client-centric ("Our focus is you. The small business owner..."). Emphasizes flat-fee (no billable hours surprise) — builds massive trust on pricing anxiety.
  - Team + contact: Attorney photo + direct email, simple form at bottom.
  - Overall: Calm, authoritative, premium without flash. Excellent "we get small biz" positioning.
- **Tone:** Professional, empathetic, straightforward. "High quality legal services at affordable prices."
- **Takeaways for Eternal:** Niche hard ( "for small local businesses" ). Flat/project pricing messaging is gold for trust. Short service lists > walls of text. Founder photo + direct contact humanizes.
- **Citations:** [web:4] Wix one-page blog.

## 3. Riggs Art Consultants (riggs-art.com) — Consultant / B2B Services
- **URL:** https://www.riggs-art.com/
- **Strengths:**
  - Clear positioning: "full-service art consulting... turn spaces into experiences."
  - Process: 3-stage (DISCOVER / DESIGN / DELIVER) with detailed descriptions — shows professionalism and reduces client risk.
  - Team: Photos + titles for 7 people (Owner, Consultants, PMs, Operations) — signals capacity + real humans.
  - Contact: Full address (NYC), phone, emails (general + specific people), "Artwork Submissions", careers note.
  - Gallery imagery throughout (even if stock) sells the "experience" outcome.
- **Tone:** Expert, collaborative, process-driven.
- **Takeaways for Eternal:** Explicit "How it Works" / process section builds confidence for custom service. Team section (even small) helps if solo + "network". Multiple contact paths. Outcome-focused language ("turn spaces into experiences" → "turn your local business into a trusted brand online").
- **Citations:** Direct fetch during session.

## General Patterns from Broader Research (Awwwards, Webflow, Framer, design sites)
- One-pagers win when: Hero is benefit + strong CTA, sections have breathing room (lots of whitespace), pricing or "next step" is clear early, mobile is flawless, load is instant.
- Trust builders that convert for local/small biz: Local location mention, named testimonials with specifics, "flat fee / project price / no surprises", process/steps, before-after or outcome stats (even placeholder "+40% inquiries"), easy contact (form + email + phone).
- Avoid: Dense text, generic stock photos without captions, "contact us" as only CTA, flashy animations that feel salesy, template-y vibes.
- Premium clean examples often use large typography, subtle purple/indigo/navy accents on white or soft dark, generous padding, sans fonts.
- Pricing visible on site (tiers or "starting at") reduces friction vs "quote only".

## Implications for Eternal Website
- Hero must have phone/email + "Get a quote" (or scroll-to-form) + benefit headline using "timeless / built to last / for your local business".
- Services: 6 cards max, icons + 1-line benefit.
- Pricing: 3 tiers visible, with "Most popular", feature comparison, flat project fee emphasis.
- Social proof: 3 testimonials (invent realistic local small-biz owners: café owner, law practice, contractor).
- Process: 4 simple steps (Discovery, Design, Build & Test, Launch & Support).
- About: Short, local (Bradenton/Sarasota FL area), "I build sites the way I wish every small business had — professional, fast, and actually useful."
- Contact: Form + direct admin@eternalwebsite.com + note "I personally reply within 24h. Or call/text [if adding number]".
- Visual: Clean, generous spacing, purple #8b3fff accent (ties to personal brand/rice without being loud), excellent typography, fast static.
- No over-promise: "one-page" focus, "perfect for most local businesses that need leads + credibility, not a 20-page corporate site."
